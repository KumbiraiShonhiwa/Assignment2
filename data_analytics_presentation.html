Introduction: This project presents an integrated data solution developed for Coffee Haven, a retail business operating both physical stores and an online ordering platform with an active loyalty programme.

Objective: The aim is to integrate POS, online, and loyalty datasets using Python and Pandas to analyse retail sales performance, compare customer behaviour across channels, and evaluate the impact of the loyalty programme on business outcomes.

Context: This analysis demonstrates the application of data integration, transformation, and analytics techniques to support strategic and tactical decision-making for non-technical business stakeholders.

Project Overview

Objectives

  • Data Integration: Combine POS, online orders, and loyalty data into unified datasets
  • Comparative Analysis: Compare in-store vs online sales across time, products, and locations
  • Loyalty Impact: Assess how loyalty programmes affect customer behavior
  • Profitability Analysis: Identify high-performing and underperforming products/stores
  • Visual Communication: Create accessible visualizations for non-technical stakeholders

Interpretation: By analyzing the relationships between in-store, online, and loyalty data, we ensured the datasets could be effectively merged to facilitate subsequent analytics. This directly supports the project's goals of assessing sales performance across different channels, examining how the loyalty program influences customer behavior, and pinpointing profitable products and stores for informed business decision-making.

Data Sources & Structure

Seven Primary Data Sources

1. Point of Sale (POS) Data

  • Transactions Line:pos_receipt_id,line_number, product, quantity, unit_price,line_discount
  • Transactions Header:store_id,trans_datetime,staff_id,payment_type,order_channel,loyalty_member_id,pos_receipt_id
  • Products

2. Online Orders Data

  • Customers: customer_id, first_name, last_name, email, phone_number, loyalty_member_id
  • Orders: online_order_id, customer_id, order_date

3. Loyalty Programme Data

  • Members: loyalty_member_id, first_name, last_name, email, phone_number
  • Points Activity: loyalty_member_id, points_txn_id

Process: We first reviewed the structure and schema of all seven data sources to understand how transactional, customer, and loyalty information is captured across systems. POS data represents in-store sales at both header and line-item level, online datasets capture e-commerce customers and orders, and loyalty datasets record member profiles and points activity. Integration is primarily enabled through shared identifiers such as loyalty_member_id, customer_id, and pos_receipt_id.

Interpretation: This structural understanding highlighted key analytical considerations, including the presence of transactions without loyalty identifiers, differences between in-store and online customer records, and varying data granularity across sources. These factors informed join strategies and ensured fair comparisons between loyalty and non-loyalty customers.

    Conclusions & Recommendations

    Key Insights

    • Channel Performance: In-store sales consistently generate higher revenue than click-and-collect across most stores, though channel contribution varies by location.
    • Time-Based Demand: Revenue peaks during early morning time ranges, with a gradual decline throughout the day, indicating time-sensitive purchasing behaviour.
    • Store Variability: Significant differences exist between store performances, with some stores consistently outperforming others across both channels.
    • Loyalty Behaviour: Loyal and non-loyal customers exhibit very similar channel preferences, suggesting loyalty influences spend rather than channel choice.

    Strategic Recommendations

    • Time-Based Optimisation: Align staffing, promotions, and inventory availability with high-performing time ranges to maximise revenue capture.
    • Store-Specific Strategies: Investigate underperforming stores to identify operational or demand-side issues, and replicate best practices from high-performing locations.
    • Channel Experience Improvement: Since channel preference is consistent across customer segments, focus on improving service quality and convenience rather than segment-specific channel targeting.
    • Performance Monitoring: Implement recurring dashboards tracking revenue by time, store, and channel to support proactive, data-driven decision-making.
